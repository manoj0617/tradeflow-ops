# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Internal employees at a wholesale and distribution company: sales staff managing leads and challans, warehouse staff maintaining products and stock, accounts staff reviewing commercial records, and administrators supervising the complete operation. They work on shared office laptops and warehouse tablets, often under time pressure, and need to understand the next safe action without training.

## Product Purpose

TradeFlow Ops connects customer follow-ups, product availability, stock movements, and sales challans in one dependable operational record. Success means an employee can move from customer intent to confirmed dispatch while the system preserves role boundaries, prevents negative stock, and makes every inventory change explainable.

## Brand Personality

Dependable, direct, and composed. The interface should feel operationally fluent rather than promotional: calm enough for daily use, explicit around irreversible actions, and confident without appearing bureaucratic.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards made from interchangeable metric-card grids.
- Decorative glassmorphism, neon gradients, oversized radii, and animated chrome.
- Consumer-shopping patterns that make inventory work feel like e-commerce.
- Dense legacy ERP screens with ambiguous abbreviations, tiny controls, or hidden state.
- Interfaces that rely on color alone to communicate stock or challan status.

## Design Principles

1. Put the operational decision before secondary information.
2. Make stock consequences visible before a user confirms an action.
3. Preserve audit context: who changed what, when, why, and from which record.
4. Keep familiar controls consistent across CRM, inventory, and challans.
5. Reveal complexity progressively while keeping primary workflows fast.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Maintain visible keyboard focus, semantic headings, labelled controls, screen-reader announcements for validation and asynchronous feedback, 44px minimum touch targets, non-color status labels, reduced-motion support, and layouts usable at 200% zoom and on 375px-wide screens.
